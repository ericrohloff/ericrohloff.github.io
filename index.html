Hi, I'm Eric Rohloff — a Computer Engineering student at Tufts University, graduating in May 2026 with minors in Computer Science and Engineering Management. I'm passionate about solving real-world problems at the intersection of embedded systems, software engineering, and computer architecture. Over the past few years, I've worked on projects ranging from building an ARM LEGv8 processor in VHDL to creating web-based data tools using BLE, MQTT, and Python. My research at Tufts has focused on extending the capabilities of the LEGO SPIKE Prime through custom hardware and software integrations with ESP32 microcontrollers. I've also served as a Residential Assistant, worked with organizations like IEEE and JumboCode, and developed embedded and full-stack applications to support both learning and community initiatives. I enjoy building things from the ground up—whether it's a PCB, a Python web server, or a digital game on an FPGA. Feel free to explore my projects or reach out if you'd like to collaborate!
